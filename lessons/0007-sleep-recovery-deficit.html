Fuel & Recover · L10

Sleep, Recovery & the Small Deficit

The work makes the signal; recovery makes the muscle. Get sleep and your calorie deficit right and you can lose fat and build muscle at the same time — your best window to do it.

⏱ ~12 minutes• 😴 With a recomp simulator• Where growth happens
◎ Why this lesson

You're in the rare sweet spot — new to lifting and carrying fat to lose — where recomposition really works. The two things that decide whether it does are sleep and the size of your calorie deficit. This lesson shows how they interact, with a simulator so you can find your own sweet spot.

1You don't grow in the gym

Training is the stimulus; the muscle is actually rebuilt during recovery.1 Protein synthesis stays elevated for 24–48 h after a session, which is why your plan leaves about 48 hours between training a muscle and spreads three sessions across the week. Rest days aren't time off from progress — they're when the progress is made. Train hard, then get out of your own way.

2Sleep is non-negotiable infrastructure

Aim for 7–9 hours. This isn't a soft "be well-rested" — short sleep directly attacks the machinery you're training:

  • One night of deprivation cut muscle protein synthesis ~18%, dropped testosterone ~24%, and raised cortisol ~21%.2
  • Dieting on short sleep is the cruel one: in a controlled study, cutting sleep from 8.5 to 5.5 h flipped weight loss the wrong way — ~55% less fat lost and ~60% more muscle lost for the same calories.3
  • Sleep loss also lowers insulin sensitivity — working directly against the metabolic-health goal from L4.
⚠ The blunt version

Under-sleep while dieting and your body burns muscle and keeps fat — the exact opposite of the plan. No supplement or training tweak out-runs this. Sleep is the first thing to fix, not the last.

3The small deficit — find your sweet spot

Because you're an ideal recomp candidate, the recipe is gentle: a small calorie deficit (~300–500 kcal/day), plenty of protein, hard training, and sleep.4 Aim to lose only about 0.5–0.7% of bodyweight a week — fast crash diets strip muscle.5 Play with the dials and watch fat loss trade off against keeping muscle:

4The plan over the year

Cut now. You have fat to spare, so a modest deficit gets you leaner and lets you build newbie muscle at the same time. Once you're noticeably leaner (around a healthy bodyweight), switch to a slight surplus — a "lean bulk", ~150–300 kcal over maintenance — to keep adding muscle with minimal fat regain.4 The recomp window is widest right now; use it.

What you can now say

  1. Muscle is built in recovery, not the gym — rest and ~48 h between sessions are part of the plan.
  2. 7–9 h sleep is infrastructure; short sleep makes you lose muscle and keep fat.
  3. For recomp: a small deficit (~300–500 kcal) + high protein + lifting + sleep.
  4. Lose only ~0.5–0.7% of bodyweight per week; faster strips muscle.
  5. Cut now while it's easy; lean-bulk later once you're leaner.
