The Lifts · L5

The Hinge & the Squat

Your two biggest lower-body lifts, built from one distinction — hips or knees — and kept entirely off your wrist.

⏱ ~14 minutes• 🏋 Cues, faults & a fault-finder• Form, wrist-safe
◎ Why this lesson

You understand the mechanics and the plan. Now the skill: performing the trap-bar deadlift and the goblet/leg-press squat well enough to load them safely and progress. Good form isn't about looking pretty — it's how you keep adding weight for years without getting hurt.

1One distinction: hips or knees

Every lower-body lift is mostly one of two patterns. Learn to tell them apart and the cues click into place:

  • A hinge (trap-bar deadlift, Romanian deadlift) is hip-dominant: hips travel back, shins stay near vertical, the torso folds forward. It trains the back of the body — glutes, hamstrings, back.
  • A squat (goblet, leg press) is knee-dominant: knees travel forward and out, the torso stays more upright. It trains the quads and glutes.

2The brace you'll use on every rep

★ The 360° brace

Before each hard rep: take a big breath into your belly (not your chest) so it expands all the way around — front, sides, low back. Brace your abs as if about to take a punch, hold the breath through the hard part, and exhale at the top.1 This stiff cylinder protects your spine under load. (If you have uncontrolled high blood pressure, clear breath-holding with a doctor and exhale through the sticking point instead.)

3The two lifts, step by step

Trap-bar deadlift main hinge

  1. Stand & grip
    Centre of the bar, feet hip-width. Use the high handles to start — neutral grip, wrists straight.
  2. Set the hips back
    Push hips back to reach the handles; shins near-vertical, chest up, flat back. Hips higher than a squat.
  3. Take the slack out
    Pull lightly up until you feel tension and your body tightens. Big brace.
  4. Push the floor away
    Drive with the legs; hips and chest rise together. Don't let the hips shoot up first.
    Lockout: stand tall, squeeze glutes — no leaning back.

Goblet squat main squat

  1. Hold it wrist-safe
    Cradle a dumbbell vertically against your chest, or a kettlebell by the horns. Wrists stacked and neutral, elbows down.
  2. Stance & brace
    Feet shoulder-width, toes out ~15–30°. 360° brace.
  3. Sit down between the hips
    Knees travel forward and out, tracking over the toes. "Spread the floor."
    Depth: thighs ~parallel, as deep as you keep a neutral spine and heels down.
  4. Drive up
    Push through the whole foot, chest up, exhale near the top.

The goblet teaches the pattern but soon gets limited by how much you can hold at your chest — that's your cue to load the squat pattern heavier on the leg press (back flat to the pad, stop before your hips curl off the seat). The Romanian deadlift is the pure hinge: soft knees fixed, push the hips straight back, feel the hamstring stretch, stand tall.

5Wrist & starting safely

✓ Wrist-safe by design

The trap-bar is ideal — neutral handles, the load just hangs, zero wrist extension. The goblet is safe held by the bell with a stacked wrist. Avoid the front-rack and low-bar back squat (both force the wrist into extension); if you ever pull from a straight bar, use straps so the grip never becomes the limit.2

⌗ First weeks: groove it light

Warm up (5 min easy cardio + bodyweight squats/hinges), then ramp up in sets. Spend the first week or two sub-maximal (3–4 reps in reserve), technique first. Add load only when reps feel easy with clean form, and back off the moment form breaks. Skill before weight.

◎ Get a real form check

You can't see your own back round. Film a side-on set and post it to a moderated community — the fastest way to fix faults: r/Fitness (daily thread) or the rehab-aware Barbell Medicine forum. Or send me a description and I'll talk you through it.

What you can now say

  1. A hinge is hip-dominant (shins vertical); a squat is knee-dominant (upright torso).
  2. Use the 360° brace on every working rep.
  3. Trap-bar: hips set high, take slack out, push the floor away — hips and chest rise together.
  4. Goblet: sit between the hips, knees out, full pain-free depth; load heavier on the leg press.
  5. Stay neutral-wrist, groove it light first, and get a form check on video.
